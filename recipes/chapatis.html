Chapatis

Chapati are a favorite food in Kenya.They are mostly made on weekends because they are a litte tedious to make.They were borrowed from the indian parantha.

Chapatis are a staple at parties, wedding and other events.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp of salt
  • 300g of wholemeal flour
  • 175ml plus 1-3 tbs vegetable oil
  • 175-225ml of water

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the salt and 250g of flour.Add 175ml of oil and mix well. Add the water, little by little, stirring after each addition, until the dough is soft. Knead the dough in the bowl for 5 to 10 minutes.
  2. Sprinkle about 25g of flour onto a flat surface.Take a 5cm ball of dough and, with a floured rolling pin,rollout into a 3mm thick circle th size of a saucer. Repeat with th remaining dough,sprinkling the flat surface with the rmaining flour if the dough sticks.

  4. Remove from the pan and drain on pieces of kitchen towel.Fry the remaining chapatis, adding more oil if necessary.
  5. Serve immediately or cover until rady to serve.
